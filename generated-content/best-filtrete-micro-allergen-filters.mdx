## What to Look for Before Buying the Best Filtrete Micro Allergen Filters

### Find Your Need - Compatibility and use case considerations

Before purchasing any air filter, you must know the exact size required by your HVAC system. Remove the existing filter and check the dimensions printed on its frame; nominal sizes (like 16x25x1) are approximate, so always verify the actual size, which is typically about 0.25 to 0.5 inches smaller. Consider where the filter will be used: a central furnace, an air conditioner, or a standalone air purifier. Some systems accommodate only certain thicknesses (e.g., 1-inch vs. 2-inch). Additionally, think about your specific needs: if you suffer from allergies, asthma, or have pets, you will want a filter with higher allergen capture, such as MERV 11 or above. For smoke or odor concerns, activated carbon filters might be better, but Filtrete Micro Allergen filters focus on particulate matter. Also, assess the airflow capacity of your system; higher MERV ratings can restrict airflow in older or weaker units, so ensure your HVAC can handle MERV 11 without strain.
