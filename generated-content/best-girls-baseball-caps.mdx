<h3>Key Features - Most important technical specifications</h3>
<p>Not all features matter equally for every child. Sun protection is non-negotiable for outdoor activities; look for UPF ratings or wide brims. Adjustability is crucial because children's heads grow; seamless adjustments like sliders or buckles outperform velcro which can snag hair. Moisture-wicking sweatbands help during sports. Prioritize features based on your child's primary use cases rather than chasing every bell and whistle.</p>

<h3>Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction</h3>
<p>Durability determines how many seasons a cap will last. Examine stitching: double-stitched seams resist unraveling better than single stitching. materials like cotton denim or premium polyester withstand repeated washes better than thin synthetics. Structured caps with internal支撑 maintain shape while unstructured caps are more packable but may become floppy. Buttons or metal eyelets at ventilation points are more durable than mere holes cut into fabric. Sweatbands should be substantial not just thin strips. Brand reputation often correlates with quality control but not always. Read reviews for mentions of shrinking fading or brim deformation. A well-made cap should survive at least one full season of regular use without significant wear.</p>

<h3>Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage</h3>
