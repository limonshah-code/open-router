### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations
Water cups range from under $15 for basic models to over $40 for premium designs with advanced features. ### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Focus on insulation type double wall vacuum insulation provides the best temperature retention. Capacity matters: 20-24 oz suits commutes while 30-40 oz serves all day needs. ### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Inspect material thickness and finish quality. Thicker stainless steel walls generally mean better insulation and durability. Seams should be smooth without rough edges. Lid mechanisms should operate smoothly without forcing. Look for warranties as they indicate manufacturer confidence. Rust resistance comes from quality stainless steel grades like 18/8. ### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Measure your car cup holder before buying if that's a primary use. Tapered designs fit better than straight cylinders. Weight matters for all day carrying: stainless steel adds heft but plastic alternatives are lighter. Handle design affects grip and portability. Consider storage space at home or in your bag. Larger cups (40+ oz) provide more hydration but may not fit everywhere. Compact 20-24 oz cups are easier to stash but require more refills. Think about your typical carrying method: handle strap bag pocket or hand. Balance capacity with practical portability based on your lifestyle.
