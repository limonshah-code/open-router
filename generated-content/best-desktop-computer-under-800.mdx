Consider software requirements—some professional applications demand specific CPU architectures or minimum RAM amounts. Also think about monitor connectivity; if you plan a dual-monitor setup ensure the desktop supports it via DisplayPort or HDMI. Matching the machine to your actual needs prevents overspending or underperforming.

### Budget - Setting realistic price expectations ($300-$800 ranges)
The under 800 category offers significant range from budget models around 300 to premium configurations near 800. At the lower end expect older processors 8GB RAM and smaller SSDs but still capable for basic tasks. The 500-600 range typically yields 6th-10th Gen CPUs 16GB RAM and 256-512GB SSDs providing excellent everyday performance. Over 600 you might find systems with 32GB RAM larger SSDs and newer processors i7 variants. Remember that refurbished business desktops often provide better specs at each price point than new consumer PCs due to corporate refresh cycles. ### Key Features - Most important technical specifications
Prioritize these features in order: RAM capacity directly impacts multitasking—aim for at least 16GB if possible. SSD storage is non-negotiable for responsiveness; avoid traditional hard drives as primary storage. Processor generation matters—10th Gen Intel or newer offer better efficiency but 6th-8th Gen remain capable. Dual monitor support is crucial for productivity; look for multiple DisplayPort or HDMI outputs. Windows 11 Pro compatibility ensures longer support and business features. Port selection including USB 3.0 and Type-C affects peripheral connectivity. For most users 16GB RAM + 512GB SSD + 6th Gen i5 represents the sweet spot for longevity.

### Quality & Durability - What to look for in construction
Business-class desktops like HP EliteDesk are built with higher quality components and better cooling than consumer models. Look for signs of thorough refurbishment: professional cleaning component testing replacement of failing parts and a genuine Windows license sticker. Check the seller's warranty—reputable refurbishers offer at least 90 days. Metal cases generally last longer than plastic. Business desktops often have superior power supplies capable of handling upgrades. Read reviews specifically about refurbishment quality; some sellers simply wipe and resell while others genuinely restore. HP's business line is known for durability and easy servicing with standard components.

### Brand Reputation - Importance of established manufacturers
Stick with established business brands like HP Dell and Lenovo for refurbished desktops. Their business lines EliteDesk ThinkCentre OptiPlex are designed for 5+ years of use with higher quality parts and better driver support. These manufacturers provide easy access to documentation service manuals and compatible upgrades. Their refurbishment programs often adhere to Microsoft Registered Refurbisher standards ensuring genuine software and proper testing. Consumer brands may use cheaper components and lack long-term driver support. HP especially dominates the refurbished business market with widespread parts availability and consistent BIOS updates even for older models.

### Portability/Size - Considerations for space and storage
Desktop form factors vary greatly. Small Form Factor SFF models like the EliteDesk 800 G2 SFF are about 10-12 liters and fit under desks easily. Mini PCs like the G3 and G6 Mini are 1-2 liters and can mount behind monitors offering truly invisible setups. Traditional towers offer more expansion room but consume more space. Consider your environment: a home office with limited desk space benefits from mini or SFF designs while a dedicated office might accommodate a tower for future upgrades. Weight matters if you need to move the unit occasionally. Most mini and SFF models weigh under 10 pounds while towers can exceed 20 pounds. Ensure your chosen location has adequate ventilation especially for compact models.
